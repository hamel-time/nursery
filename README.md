
## About the Author

**Ryan Hamel** is based in Montréal, QC. 

My writing focuses on the intersection of **non-hierarchical metaphysics** - the nature of the universe without the use of unquestionable, preordained existence - and how hierarchical metaphysics and philosophy have shaped the mental models of those that live as subjects to empires. 

This intersection is inherently transdisciplinary, but a constant theme will be relational existence and emergent forms; from **physics** to **hockey** to **solving global warming** to **universal grammar** to **solving loneliness**. I do my best to learn from **Indigenous** and **non-Western scholars**, but ground my work **within Western traditions**. My tone will often be more sermonal and conversational, except for my metaphysics series of papers found under philosophy/.

When I'm not writing, I'm usually with my family or listening to interviews or lectures from an interesting person.

I am also a Day 1 season-ticket holder for **Victoire de Montréal** and an obsessed fan of the **Canadiens de Montréal**.

Lastly, I am an American who is becoming Québécois and Canadian.

**AI Usage**  

Generally I write all of my essays without anything but reader feedback from an LLM model. However, as I am not a trained philosopher and my tone would clash with the field, I found it incredibly useful to use an LLM model to help me with not just feedback but cleaning up my prose. So yes, there are em-dashes galore under philosophy/ but I will not use AI in the same way in other sections. I am using Claude Opus 4.6 for the bulk of it, though I often use the free versions of other models as adversarial feedback as well.
