## Background: What Is Metaphysics?

When you were a child, and you asked your parents "*Why is the sky blue?"*", you were already studying metaphysics in that moment.

Eventually your parents likely got frustrated and said "it just is".

Metaphysics is for the people who never accepted "it just is". For our purposes here, that is a good enough definition.

[Relevant Wikipedia entry.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metaphysics)  

## How I Could Not Avoid Becoming A Metaphysician

The alarm goes off in my bedroom. I am a teenager - not old enough to drive, probably around 14 or 15 years old. I am partly awake, partly asleep, and the implications of the *PBS Spacetime* or *NOVA* show I watched the night before are still ringing in my head, just as loud as this electronically produced squawk.

While considering my options - 
*to get up or not to get up....* - I realized I had to weigh the consequences of sleeping just a bit longer...  I drifted from typical grounded human concerns - a recurring pattern throughout my life, breakfast, basic self-care, enjoying the morning etc - I had a much more interesting thought instead on how my choice would affect the world at large.

The world? The world would be affected by my decision to sleep in or not? 

How dramatic. 

I mean, to be fair, I was a teenager.

Still, I should have been more precise. I was not talking about **this world** - I was talking about **Jupiter**. Or any planet, really, but Jupiter is just so friggin massive and huge, the idea that I could have **any** effect on Jupiter is what made my mind really glow with hot excitement in this liminal space between the warmth of my bed and the cold of a January morning in Western Massachusetts.

How, dear reader, was I going to affect Jupiter, you may ask? **Well, let's talk about gravity.**

**Gravity**, as you may or may not know, is a force of nature that attracts mass towards other mass[^gravity].

So gravity is a force where mass attracts mass. Stuff attracts stuff. 

You right now are stuck on this rock in space because this rock in space is made of lots of stuff, lots of mass. The stuff inside you, also having mass, is attracted to it and keeps you here on Earth, rather than letting you get a nice up close look at Starlink satellites burning up in our atmosphere.

The real tricky concept with gravity, however, is that it is not just that the earth is pulling you down - you are also pulling the earth up. Granted, you are losing very badly at this cosmological game of tug-of-war, but the **stuff inside you has no quit** - which makes sense, as it is **quite literally made up of several generations of exploded stars.** Stardust has survived billions of years playing this game, and it isn't giving up now.

So the cheap Radio Shack (they are gone now, I can diss them right?) alarm is blaring, my stomach is growling, and I am considering this simple fact:

> If I use my free will[^freewill] to hit this snooze button and get up in 15 minutes **or** I decide to get up right now - there will be a change in the distribution of matter - a tiny jiggling of the stuff - that will ever-so-slightly have an effect on Jupiter.  
> 
> Eventually.  
> 
> Also, that tiny jiggling of the stuff I do now will make a tiny tug on Jupiter, which in turn will change Jupiter's tug on my stuff.  
> 
> Eventually.  
> 
> And so on, and so on, until I die, and really, even beyond **that**.  
> 
> I may quit but my stardust won't.
> 
> In fact, *the entire universe will never be the same* because I decided to hit the snooze button **or** get up and eat breakfast.  

The atoms inside of my body will come to a decision - get up or do not get up - and then the impact of this decision will be felt by Jupiter when the decision has had enough time to reach it. I will forever alter the orbit of Jupiter with this seemingly simple decision, but one made up entirely of me and my atoms and the emergent free will encapsulated in this sack of flesh we call a human being; snooze or get up.

That thought experiment has been haunting me my entire life. Not that every decision I make some how alters the cosmos, forever - no, that is fucking amazing if completely uncontrollable and chaotic. That thought brings me joy and optimism even in my darkest hours, which I have had more than my fair share.

No, what has been haunting me my entire life is this - how the **fuck** does the universe keep track of all of that? How does the universe both allow for sentient life to come up with the bright idea to **ride a lawnmower down a waterslide and also have the impact of that decision echo back and forth throughout the cosmos**, accurately adjusting the tug felt by every object in the cosmos because of that decision?

Did I ever find an answer to that thought experiment? If you know who *Alfred North Whitehead* is, you can take a stab at reading my series of essays on a revision of Process Theory (still a work-in-progress as of 2026-03-07). If you have no idea who that is, I will eventually be producing something easier to digest.

Also, not to leave you in suspense: what was my decision, dear reader?  

Well, my mom went shopping and I realized I had to run down and eat the good cereal before my sisters did.

Duh.

And because of my mom, Jupiter and I have just never been the same since.

## Afterword

This thought experiment has haunted me my entire life, and has driven me to get an answer. It has been ~28 years and I have an answer that satisfies my questions. Will they satisfy yours? Will they satisfy anyone else's? I am not sure, but my goal in this next phase of my life as a writer is to get these ideas out of my head, these ideas that have grown and grown from that single seed of a question, and see if anyone else thinks what grew from that seed is just a weed or a flower.

In the meantime, the next time the alarm goes off, remember that the whole universe depends on your decision.
