title: An Empireless Universe
subtitle: A Serial Process Metaphysics
author: 
  - Ryan Hamel
  - Montréal, QC
abstract: |
 Alfred North Whitehead's process philosophy provides the most developed relational ontology in the Western tradition, yet nearly a century of process thought has largely declined to use it for its most ambitious purpose: a unified account of the physical world. This paper takes up that project. Starting from a single primitive — the prehensive act — and a serial architecture derived from radical relationalism, I propose five interconnected arguments. First, *pausality*: satisfaction and transition are identical, meaning concrescence cannot reach satisfaction without a downstream occasion creating the relational demand for its completion, providing an ontological account of the measurement problem. Second, *energy as causal activity*: what physics measures as energy is the prehensive output of occasions, an identification from which time, mass, gravity, and holography emerge as consequences — demonstrating that the same framework that grounds quantum determination also grounds gravitational phenomena. Third, *a relational cosmogony*: the first act is differentiation from absence, freedom is the first relational discovery, and everything Whitehead attributed to eternal objects and God's primordial nature is accounted for as accumulated relational discovery. What Whitehead calls eternal, I call ancient. Fourth, *exceptionless cosmology*: if forms are relational discoveries, so are the forces of nature, including gravity as the first and most fundamental discovery — with implications for dark matter, fine-tuning, cosmic inflation, and the cosmological constant. Fifth, *an empireless metaphysics*: a universe with no transcendent authority provides a different mental model for how a species understands itself and its possibilities. Together these five arguments constitute a process-relational ontology in which measurement, gravitation, the origin of form, cosmological structure, and human self-understanding are aspects of a single dynamic — the propagation and integration of prehension. The framework presupposes a serial universe — one prehensive act at a time, with all apparent simultaneity emergent. I offer this as a coherent philosophical proposal, not a physical theory. It lacks formalization and makes no quantitative predictions. It is, I hope, at least worth proving wrong.

\begin{center}
\section*{Reader's Guide}
\end{center}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Reader's Guide}

This paper proposes a process metaphysics built from a single primitive — the prehensive act, one occasion entering into relation with another — and derives from it accounts of quantum mechanics, gravity, the origin of form, cosmological structure, and human self-understanding. It engages seriously with Whitehead's process philosophy but departs from it substantially. This guide summarizes the major commitments and moves so the reader knows what is coming.

**What this paper is.** Speculative metaphysics. It does not write equations, derive predictions, or claim to replace any physical formalism. Its value, if any, is in the coherence and parsimony of the unified picture. It is offered whole — from quantum mechanics to human self-understanding — because the coherence of the complete picture is itself evidence worth presenting.

**About the author.** The author is not an academic, and arrived to a metaphysical framework through a combination of insights from distributed systems and a lifelong thought experiment about how one would create the universe from first principles, or in the author's field — a "white room implementation" . The author for much of his life did not fully understand that this thought experiment was metaphysics itself, so by the time he came to Whitehead much of the two frameworks already agreed. The author hopes that, as he believes Whitehead would agree, that good metaphysics is discoverable from many paths. Still, the author has blind spots that academic training would have prevented, and acknowledges that this work requires engagement with scholars better equipped to identify what has been missed.

**The starting point.** All properties are constitutively relational. Nothing exists except in relation. Self-relation — an occasion's relation to its own prehensive process — is genuine relation. This is not argued for — it is the framework's foundational axiom, stated as such. Readers who reject radical relationalism will reject what follows. Readers who accept it, even provisionally, are invited to see what it produces.

**The architecture.** The universe is fundamentally serial. One prehensive act occurs at a time. All apparent simultaneity — including spacetime itself — is emergent. This is derived from radical relationalism plus a well-established result from distributed systems theory: any parallel system with real communication costs is observationally equivalent to a serial execution, and under radical relationalism, observational equivalence is ontological equivalence.

**The load-bearing revision.** Prehension is revised from the uptake of completed data to the act of entering into relation. This revision is more consequential than any other move in the paper and is the true foundation of the framework. When applied to a satisfied occasion, prehension is ordinary inheritance of data. When applied to an unsatisfied occasion, prehension is the downstream demand that triggers its completion. Same act, different consequences depending on the state of the prehended occasion.

**The core claim.** Satisfaction (an occasion becoming determinate) and transition (an occasion becoming datum for another) are not two sequential events. They are identical — the same event described from two perspectives. This means an occasion cannot become determinate without a downstream occasion to receive the result. Determination is demand-driven. This is called *pausality*.

**What pausality produces.** An ontological account of quantum measurement (determination occurs when a downstream occasion creates relational demand), entanglement (a partially coalesced society of occasions completed by measurement), and wave-particle duality (unsatisfied occasions have expanding relational possibilities; satisfied occasions are determinate events).

**Energy.** What physics measures as energy is causal activity — the prehensive output of occasions. This is an ontological identification, not a metaphor. From it: time is the cost of integrating prehension (more causal history to integrate means slower concrescence means slower time). Mass is the cost of maintaining internal topology across public relational channels. Gravity emerges from the gradient of accumulated causal history. The same primitive that grounds quantum determination grounds gravitational phenomena. One process, no seam.

**The origin of everything.** The first act is differentiation from absence, constituting both existence and non-existence. This is the one primitive the framework does not derive — the one brute fact at the bottom of the system. Prehension, in this framework, is entering into relation with what is relationally available. For the first occasion, only absence was available. For every subsequent occasion, both absence and other occasions are available. The first act is not a different kind of act — it is the same act with minimal input. Freedom is the first relational discovery — it comes into existence when differentiation from absence exhausts itself and a second path appears. Everything Whitehead attributed to eternal objects (the forms that give occasions their definite character) is accounted for as accumulated relational discovery — inherited forward through causal history, ancient but not eternal. What Whitehead calls eternal, I call ancient. Beauty is what produces more relational possibility downstream — natural selection applied to forms rather than organisms.

**God.** God is not an entity but an act: the act of choosing novelty over repetition, performed by every occasion. The revisions to God proposed here do not remove the divine from the framework. They distribute it — making the universe not less divine but more so. Cosmic unity is architectural, not theological — under the serial constraint, one prehensive act occurs at a time, meaning the universe is always unified in the experience of whatever occasion is currently relating. Occasions that never find a downstream partner concresce indefinitely, accumulating the universe's prehensive history without ever completing. No value is destroyed. An occasion's experience of its own value is sufficient unto itself. To demand an audience is to impose a hierarchy the framework rejects.

**What this means for cosmology.** If forms are relational discoveries, so are the forces of nature. Gravity is the first discovery — the discovery that prehending causal history is more generative than ignoring it. The other forces are subsequent discoveries, relational configurations found as the universe complexified. Dark matter is topology that discovered gravity but not the subsequent forces. Cosmic inflation is what happens when a young universe has almost no causal history to integrate — differentiation from absence is fast because there is almost nothing else to do. The cosmological constant decreases as the total number of prehensive acts grows.

**What this means for us.** A universe with no transcendent authority — no eternal objects, no primordial divine nature, no pre-given forms — provides a different mental model than one in which unquestionable givens are the foundation of reality. If isolation is ontologically impoverished and relation is existence, then the individualist mental model is not merely a bad strategy. It is a misdescription of what you are.

\begin{center}
\section*{Introduction}
\end{center}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Introduction}

Whitehead intended process philosophy to be a cosmology — a comprehensive account of the nature of reality that would encompass physics, biology, consciousness, and value within a single metaphysical scheme. *Process and Reality* is explicit about this ambition. Yet the subsequent tradition has largely pursued Whitehead's insights in theology, ecology, and philosophy of mind while leaving the physics on the table.[^1] This is understandable. Whitehead's own engagement with physics was shaped by the science of the 1920s. The measurement problem had not yet matured into its modern form. Bell's theorem was decades away. The holographic principle, entropic gravity, and quantum information theory did not exist. Whitehead had the metaphysical primitives but lacked the physical phenomena that would most clearly demonstrate their power.

This paper attempts to pick up where Whitehead's physics left off — and then to follow the implications beyond physics into cosmogony, cosmology, and the question of what this means for how we live. I propose that his core primitive — prehension, the process by which one actual occasion takes account of another — is sufficient to ground a unified ontological account of domains that current physics and philosophy treat separately: the transition from indeterminate to determinate, the nature of time, mass, and gravitation, the origin of form and novelty, the large-scale structure of the universe, and the relationship between metaphysics and human self-understanding. The argument is entirely metaphysical. I do not write equations, derive predictions, or claim to replace any existing physical formalism. What I offer is an ontology — an account of what is actually happening underneath the equations — and an argument that this particular ontology is more unified, more parsimonious, and more consistent with the relational character of modern physics than the alternatives currently on offer.

One further commitment must be stated at the outset because it shapes the entire framework that follows. This paper presupposes that **the universe is fundamentally serial** — one prehensive act at a time, and all apparent simultaneity is emergent. The argument is developed in Section 1.2. Readers familiar with Whitehead's massively parallel cosmology, in which countless occasions concresce simultaneously across the extensive continuum, should note that this is not a revision but a replacement of that architecture — simultaneity is an emergent experience, not ontological.

**A note on the structure of this paper.** The paper is structured in five parts. Their overarching goals are:

1. **Quantum Mechanics** — Pausality entails an ontology for measurement, entanglement, and wave-particle duality.
2. **Gravity** — The same primitive defines energy, time, mass, and then gravity. The primary aim is to demonstrate that quantum determination and gravitational phenomena emerge from a single relational process.
3. **Cosmogony** — A self-grounding origin story requiring no external ontology. Forms, freedom, beauty, and God are accounted for as relational discoveries.
4. **Cosmology** — The framework applied to open problems: forces as relational discoveries, dark matter, fine-tuning, cosmic inflation, and the cosmological constant.
5. **Humanity** — What a universe with no transcendent authority means for how a species understands itself.

Parts 1 and 2 can each stand on their own as contributions to process philosophy and the philosophy of physics respectively. Part 3 completes the metaphysical framework. Part 4 is explicitly speculative — the framework applied to open cosmological problems, presented as sketches rather than developed accounts. Part 5 explores what the framework means for human self-understanding. Together, I believe the coherence of the complete picture is itself evidence worth presenting, and I would rather offer the framework whole and invite others to formalize, critique, or demolish it than publish the safe part and keep the rest in a drawer.

A note on the theological character of these revisions. The changes proposed to God's role in the framework do not remove the divine from the system. They distribute it. What Whitehead attributed to a single exceptional entity, this paper argues is a feature of every occasion — making the universe not less divine but more so. The full argument is developed in Parts 1 and 3.

The revisions proposed here are substantial. They include a serial architecture that replaces Whitehead's parallel cosmology, a relational account of satisfaction that revises his privacy/publicity distinction, the identification of energy with causal activity, a cosmogony grounded in differentiation from absence, a rejection of eternal objects in favour of inherited relational discoveries, and a reconception of God as act rather than entity. I flag these departures at the outset because they are deliberate, and because I believe they follow from Whitehead's own relational commitments more naturally than the positions they replace.

Process philosophy, if it is true, applies to itself. *Process and Reality* is not an eternal object — it is an occasion in a historical nexus. Whitehead inherited the full weight of Western philosophy, including a Platonism he could not entirely metabolize within his own concrescence. The eternal objects bear the mark of this inheritance: they sit uneasily within a system that otherwise grounds existence in relation, not in form. To notice this tension is not to diminish Whitehead but to take him seriously. As Isabelle Stengers has argued,[^2] the point is to think *with* Whitehead, not to treat his system as a closed text requiring only exegesis.

A process philosophy that cannot be prehended and transformed by its successors has become the very thing it argues against — a static form, immune to novelty. This paper proceeds in the spirit Whitehead himself described: the many become one, and are increased by one.

### 1.1 The Two Species of Process

Whitehead distinguishes two species of process: concrescence and transition. Concrescence is the internal becoming of an actual occasion — the process by which an occasion prehends its world, integrates what it receives, and arrives at a determinate satisfaction. Transition is the passage from one completed occasion into the data of another — the process by which a perished occasion becomes objective datum for subsequent occasions. These are not sequential phases where one is primary and the other derivative. Whitehead treats them as mutually necessary, co-fundamental aspects of how reality advances.


### 1.2 The Serial Constraint

A well-established result in distributed systems theory holds that any parallel system with real communication costs is observationally equivalent to a serial execution — the outputs are indistinguishable[^3]. This is not a limitation of observation. In a relational ontology where all properties are constitutively relational, observational equivalence is ontological equivalence. If two architectures produce identical relational structures, they are the same architecture. There is no hidden intrinsic property that distinguishes 'really parallel' from 'really serial.' Parsimony then favors the simpler description.

The fundamental unit of this serial process is the single prehensive act — one occasion integrating one prehension. This is the smallest unit of being. An occasion's concrescence comprises many such acts, and multiple occasions may be mid-concrescence at any point — accumulating prehensions, growing in internal richness. What cannot occur is two prehensive acts at once. The universe advances one act of relation at a time. This inverts Whitehead's ontological principle, in which the actual occasion is the fundamental unit. In the present framework, occasions are constituted by prehensive acts, making relations more fundamental than relata. This is consistent with radical relationalism and will be developed further in subsequent work.

This means there is no contemporaneity in the fundamental causal structure. Two occasions that appear simultaneous from within spacetime are interleaved in the serial order of prehensive acts. Spacetime simultaneity is what this serial process looks like from inside — an emergent description, not a fundamental feature. The extensive continuum, which in Whitehead's system grounds the possibility of simultaneous becoming, is in the present framework an emergent structure produced by the accumulated history of prehensive acts. This departs from Whitehead's cosmological architecture, in which countless occasions concresce simultaneously. In the present framework, that simultaneity is appearance, not ontology.

The serial constraint is not an incidental feature of this framework. It is architecturally foundational. The unit of seriality is also the unit of temporality — as developed in Part 2, time just is the count of prehensive acts. Pausality — the claim that satisfaction requires a downstream occasion — is simpler under seriality: satisfaction occurs when another occasion's prehensive act creates the relational demand. The preferred causal frame discussed in Section 1.8 is not merely a feature of entanglement but a consequence of the serial architecture — there is a total order on all prehensive acts, and spacetime is what that order looks like from inside. The serial order is not itself a source of indeterminacy or determinism — causally dependent acts have a forced ordering, and causally independent acts commute. The question of freedom pertains to the content of each act, not to the sequence. The serial constraint will be presupposed throughout.

### 1.3 Transition Requires a Receiver

Transition is not an occasion broadcasting into the void. It is the process by which a perished occasion becomes objective datum *for* a subsequent occasion. The "for" is constitutive, not incidental. In a relational ontology, a datum that is datum for nothing is not a datum. Without a prehending occasion, transition has no content. This is likely a controversial addition to Whitehead — though I argue that it follows directly from the relational character of his metaphysics.

### 1.4 Concrescence Cannot Close Unilaterally

Here is the central claim. If transition is co-fundamental with concrescence, and transition requires a receiver, then an occasion that reaches satisfaction with no downstream occasion to prehend the result has undergone concrescence without transition — only half of process. The occasion is not suspended in some liminal state, nor is it waiting in ontological quarantine. It is fully underway: concrescing, prehending, integrating, exercising full autonomy over its internal process. What it cannot do is *complete*. Satisfaction — the moment of determinateness and perishing into objective datum — is contingent on there being a relational context for that completion. Concrescence has genuine duration, and that duration extends until a downstream occasion creates the demand for the result.

A clarification is necessary here. I am not claiming that concrescence and transition are two separate processes that happen to require each other. I am claiming they are the same event described from two vantage points. Satisfaction — the moment an occasion becomes determinate — *is* the moment it becomes datum for a downstream occasion. These are not sequential: first becoming determinate, then becoming available. Becoming determinate and becoming relational datum are *identical*. An occasion cannot reach satisfaction without transition because satisfaction without transition is not an incomplete version of process — it is a description of something that does not correspond to any possible event. This goes beyond Whitehead's own formulation, in which the two species are co-fundamental but not identified.

Consider the alternative. If an occasion could reach satisfaction without any downstream occasion to inherit the result, its determinateness would make no relational difference. In a relational ontology, determinateness that produces no possible relational difference is indistinguishable from indeterminateness. This is a commitment of the framework, not a self-evident truth. It follows from radical relationalism — the position that all properties are constitutively relational. A metaphysician who holds that intrinsic, non-relational properties exist would reject this principle. The argument that follows depends on it.

Self-relation — the occasion's relation to its own prehensive process — is genuine relation within this framework. Radical relationalism does not privilege external relation over internal. The occasion before and after prehending an act is a differentiation, and there exists a relation between the two.

**The occasion does not decide it is done. It is done when something needs it to be done.**

I call this *pausality*. A clarification on the word itself. *Pausality* is an emergent description, not an ontological claim about the occasion's own experience. From the occasion's perspective, nothing pauses — it undergoes exactly as many prehensions as it undergoes. The "pause" only appears from a vantage point that assumes temporal homogeneity across regions — what I call *time arrogance*, the assumption that all regions of the universe have experienced the same number of occasions. An occasion that has not yet entered into downstream relation is not frozen. It simply has not yet been inherited forward. Put most precisely: an occasion is always in relation to another occasion — the question is whether it is in relation to a *downstream* occasion. It is constituted by its antecedent relations; it is just not yet constitutive of subsequent ones.

If time is derivative of process — if the passage of time just is the completion of concrescences — then temporal homogeneity across regions is an emergent statistical regularity, not a metaphysical guarantee. A causal mechanism for time may produce apparent homogeneity in the aggregate while permitting individual occasions that have undergone significantly fewer concrescences than their neighbours. To assume that the aggregate prohibits outliers is to mistake a statistical tendency for a law.

A further distinction is necessary. The downstream occasion's role is to trigger the completion of concrescence — to create the relational demand that allows satisfaction. Prehension, meanwhile, accumulates freely throughout the occasion's duration without constraint. The occasion is not waiting in an empty room. It is mid-process, gathering and integrating prehensions, with its internal richness growing. What it lacks is the condition for closure. The trigger and the prehensive accumulation are independent.

Whether the occasion's integration of its prehensions is fully determined by its inheritance or whether genuine freedom exists in the act of integration is a question addressed in subsequent papers. The present paper requires only that satisfaction is demand-driven, not that the content of satisfaction is predetermined.

### 1.4.1 On Retrocausality And Public vs Private
An objection arises: in Whitehead's system, an occasion is private while concrescing and becomes public only at satisfaction. How then can a downstream occasion prehend an unsatisfied occasion? The answer requires revising the privacy/publicity distinction itself. If satisfaction is relational rather than unilateral, then the sharp boundary between private concrescence and public datum softens. An unsatisfied occasion is not hidden from the universe. It is mid-process, constituted by antecedent relations, accumulating prehensions — it is relationally present. A downstream occasion does not read the unsatisfied occasion's subjective content. The act of establishing relation is identical with the satisfaction that makes the content available. The relation does not access the process; it completes it. It needs only to prehend it — and prehension, in a relational ontology, is not retrieval of a completed datum but the act of entering into relation. The demand is not a signal from the future. It is the present act of a new occasion reaching toward what is relationally available.

This revision to the concept of prehension — from the uptake of completed data to the act of entering into relation — is arguably more consequential than pausality itself, and will be developed fully in subsequent work.

This redefinition is not optional within the framework. If satisfaction is relational — if an occasion cannot become determinate without a downstream occasion creating relational demand — then the downstream occasion must be able to enter into relation with an unsatisfied occasion. Prehension defined as uptake of completed data makes this impossible, because the data is not yet complete. Prehension defined as entering into relation makes it natural. The redefinition is entailed by pausality itself.

### 1.5 Empirical Consistency

The distinction between a trigger that resolves and prehension that accumulates is not merely conceptual. In the famous Bell tests, a quantum state is created in superposition — the indeterminate state — and only reaches a determinate state upon measurement. A Bell test, however, could still be conceived of metaphysically as a 'frozen' collection of occasions that only 'unfreeze' when needed. A more complete example are spin echo experiments. In a spin echo experiment, a quantum system appears to lose coherence — its phase information seemingly dispersed — only for a refocusing pulse to recover the coherent state, demonstrating that the system was accumulating and maintaining internal structure throughout the period of apparent indeterminacy. Whatever metaphysics one brings to such experiments, any account of concrescence must be consistent with this kind of physical behaviour: systems that retain and build internal richness through apparent indeterminacy, completed by a downstream interaction. Had Whitehead had access to spin echo experiments alongside the Bell tests that were still decades away, the path from his relational ontology to demand-driven concrescence would have been considerably shorter.

### 1.6 God's Consequent Nature and the Primordial Nature

Whitehead introduced God's consequent nature in part to guarantee that no occasion perishes without being taken up — that the universe is, in a sense, lossless. Every occasion that perishes is prehended by God, preserving its contribution. This is elegant, but if concrescence itself cannot complete without a downstream prehension partner, the problem God's consequent nature was introduced to solve does not arise. There are no orphaned occasions to rescue. The relational structure of process prevents them from occurring in the first place. God's consequent nature performs other functions in Whitehead's system beyond preventing orphaned occasions — including the preservation of achieved value, the integration of incompatible experiences, and the ground of everlasting significance. These are substantial roles, and the full implications of removing God's consequent nature from them are addressed in subsequent papers in this series. The present paper addresses only the structural role most directly relevant to pausality.

I want to be explicit that this is a significant revision of Whitehead, not a strict derivation from his premises. Whitehead did not make this move, and I am not claiming he was secretly committed to it. What I am claiming is that the revision cuts toward his core commitments rather than away from them. In a relational ontology, occasions cannot close unilaterally; satisfaction is constitutively social. Whitehead lacked the measurement problem in its modern form — the question of what triggers the transition from indeterminate to determinate had not yet crystallized into the shape that makes pausality visible as a candidate answer. This is a historical limitation, not a gap in the coherence of his system. Whitehead was doing a specific job: reconciling metaphysics with the physics of his moment. It has been nearly a century. The aim here is to prehend what that century has produced — across physics, the sciences, and the broader project of understanding what reality is — and let that inheritance drive process theory toward a new concrescence.

### 1.7 Permanent Concrescence

Not every occasion will find a downstream partner. Under the serial constraint, this does not halt the universe — the occasion simply continues accumulating prehensive acts, interleaved with everything else, never reaching satisfaction. Its experience continues. Its prehensive richness grows. It is not frozen, not lost, not deficient. It is permanently becoming.

This is structurally analogous to what Whitehead described as God's consequent nature — an entity that prehends the universe's ongoing output without ever completing. The difference is that in the present framework, this is not a unique divine entity but a possible condition of any occasion that happens never to be inherited forward. Permanent concrescence is relationally contingent, not theologically guaranteed.

No value is destroyed in this framework. An occasion that never finds a downstream partner never becomes public datum — its experience is never inherited forward as data for the rest of the universe. But its experience is real. The occasion relates to its own prehensive process, and self-relation is genuine relation. To insist that value only counts when it has an audience — when it is inherited by something external — is to impose a hierarchy among occasions that the framework rejects. There are no special occasions. The occasion's experience of its own value is sufficient unto itself.

### 1.8 Pausality and Measurement

Whitehead describes concrescence as reaching "satisfaction" through its own internal process but does not specify what determines *when* satisfaction occurs. In standard quantum mechanics, an indeterminate system becomes determinate upon measurement, but the theory does not say what constitutes a measurement or why it has this effect. Pausality provides a unified answer: an occasion reaches satisfaction when a downstream occasion requires it as prehension. Determination is demand-driven. Measurement is not a special category of interaction, and observation is not metaphysically privileged. Any occasion that creates relational demand for the result of another occasion's concrescence triggers its completion. The measurement problem was generated by treating determination as something that happens *to* a system from outside, when it is in fact the natural completion of a relational process.

The account offered here does not map cleanly onto any existing interpretation of quantum mechanics. It shares with Rovelli's relational quantum mechanics the conviction that determination is relational rather than absolute[^4], but differs in providing a specific trigger condition — relational demand from a downstream occasion — that relational QM leaves unspecified. It shares with causal realism the commitment to a real causal structure independent of observers. It is incompatible with interpretations that deny a unique outcome (many-worlds) or that treat quantum states as subjective (QBism). If pausality is correct, it constitutes a distinct interpretation: determination is neither collapse imposed by observation, nor branching into parallel outcomes, nor subjective updating of beliefs. It is the natural completion of a relational process.[^5]

### 1.9 Entanglement and the No-Signaling Constraint

Pausality has a further implication that deserves separate treatment. The no-signaling theorem in quantum mechanics — the result that entanglement cannot be used for faster-than-light communication — is well established formally. The reduced density matrix of either subsystem is unchanged by a distant measurement; the math is clear. What the formalism does not provide is an ontological account of *why* the universe is structured this way. Pausality offers one.

Consider a standard Bell test. Two entangled particles are separated and measured at distant locations. The standard account says their correlations are non-local but cannot be exploited for signaling. The question is why. The usual answer appeals to the formalism: the statistics at each wing are locally random regardless of what happens at the other. This is correct but ontologically silent. It describes the constraint without explaining its source.

Within pausality, entanglement is not best understood as two separate particles that happen to be correlated. It is an occasion[^6] that has constituted both wings without yet reaching satisfaction. The two wings are relational neighbours not because they are spatially proximate but because they share an unsatisfied occasion. Their "non-locality" is a spatial category error: occasions are individuated relationally, not geometrically. 

A note on the relational structure of the entangled system. The entity constituting both wings is not static — it is a society of occasions in partial coalescence. A partially coalesced society is one in which some constituent occasions have reached satisfaction while others remain unsatisfied, awaiting downstream partners. Each wing represents a site where unsatisfied occasions within the entity can find their downstream relation. When measurement occurs at one wing, it provides the downstream relation that the remaining unsatisfied occasions on that side require. Crucially, the prehensive content of the measuring occasion — the measurement context — becomes part of what those occasions integrate into their satisfaction. The other wing then inherits the result as prehension from the now-satisfied entity. The correlation between the wings is not transmitted between them. It emerges from shared prehensive history within the entity, shaped by the specific content contributed by whichever wing completed the coalescence.

A clarification on what constitutes relational demand. Not every prehension of an unsatisfied occasion triggers its satisfaction. A photon passing through the causal neighborhood of an entangled system prehends whatever satisfied occasions are available and continues — it has no open relation that requires the entangled system's completion. A measurement apparatus is different. Its occasions are configured such that their own concrescence specifically requires the entangled system's result as input — they cannot proceed without it. This is what makes relational demand specific rather than promiscuous. The trigger is not any downstream prehension. It is a downstream occasion whose own relational structure has an open slot that only this unsatisfied occasion's completion can fill. Demand is structural need, not generic contact.

When measurement occurs, the occasion is satisfied. Both wings are at causal distance 1 from that occasion — they are direct inheritors, not recipients of a traveling signal. There is no propagation event. Satisfaction is not something that moves from one wing to the other at infinite speed; there is no *it* that moves. The occasion was constituting both wings throughout; its satisfaction is a relational fact for both at once. Not instantaneous transfer — structurally non-sequential.

The framework provides structural resources — measurement-context integration and shared prehensive history — that are the right kind of mechanism to account for Bell correlations, since outcomes are constituted in the act of satisfaction rather than pre-determined. This contextuality — outcomes constituted in measurement rather than revealed by it — is consistent with the Kochen-Specker theorem's demonstration that quantum mechanics cannot assign pre-existing values to all observables simultaneously. The framework rejects outcome independence: the outcome at each wing is partly constituted by the shared prehensive history of the entity, and is therefore not independent of the outcome at the other wing. This places pausality outside the scope of Bell's theorem, which assumes outcome independence. Whether these structural resources can reproduce the specific angular dependence of quantum correlations remains an open question requiring formalization.

The no-signaling result is then structurally accounted for. Signaling requires a directed causal chain: A influences B influences C. What entanglement produces is a fork — two occasions each at causal distance 1 from the same satisfied parent, neither downstream of the other. There is no directed path between the wings along which a signal could travel. Once the shared occasion is satisfied, the relationship that made the wings neighbours has perished. The occasion can only give prehension to non-satisfied occasions — it cannot reach backward or form new relationships with already-completed occasions. To establish a new causal connection between the wings, you would need new relational contact, which is just ordinary classical causation subject to ordinary spatial constraints.

The appearance of paradox is, once again, *time arrogance*: the assumption that the two wings occupy a shared temporal sequence and that the measurement result must somehow "catch up" from one to the other. They do not share a temporal sequence. They share a relational structure — the unsatisfied occasion that constituted both. Once that occasion is satisfied and perishes, the wings are genuinely separated. New occasions, no inherited relationship, nothing to exploit. The non-locality in spacetime was real but singular: one unsatisfied occasion, one satisfaction, one fork. It is a one-time relational fact — local in causal terms, non-local only from the perspective of spacetime. It is not a channel that persists. It is worth stressing - as far as both of the wings are concerned, the quantum occasion is a local relational fact whose potential relationship was formed at the moment of entanglement - when both wings were, in fact, local in spacetime.

A note on causal ordering. The serial constraint introduced in Section 1.2 entails a total order on all prehensive acts. In a Bell test, this means one wing's prehensive acts reach the unsatisfied occasions in the society before the other's — there is a definite causal sequence, not merely a preferred frame. The prehension structure has a definite sequence — one wing triggers satisfaction before the other. This is not a spatial or temporal fact but a causal one: the order in which occasions enter into relation is a feature of the relational structure itself. This constitutes a preferred frame of reference, but it is a frame that exists in the causal structure, not in spacetime. From within spacetime, this ordering is not recoverable — there is no measurement that could determine which wing triggered satisfaction first, because the distinction is not a spatiotemporal one. The framework is therefore compatible with Lorentz invariance. Relativity is not violated because relativity describes the structure of spacetime, and the preferred causal frame is not a spatiotemporal frame. It is the underlying relational ordering from which spacetime is derived. The physicist's concern — that any preferred frame must conflict with special relativity — assumes that all frames are spatiotemporal. In a framework where spacetime is emergent from causal structure rather than fundamental, the assumption does not hold.

### 1.10 Wave-Particle Duality

Pausality reframes wave-particle duality — not by explaining how something can be both wave and particle, but by showing that the distinction was never ontological in the first place.

An unsatisfied occasion has an expanding set of potential downstream occasions — possible relational partners that could trigger its satisfaction. This set is not a thing in space. It is the relational possibilities available to the occasion. As the occasion persists without satisfaction, nearby occasions continue to reach their own satisfaction and new occasions emerge, so the set of potential partners keeps shifting and expanding. From a spacetime perspective, this expanding set of potential relations *is* wave behaviour. The occasion is not "in many places at once." It has many potential relational partners at once.

When one of those potential partners commits — when a downstream occasion creates the relational demand for the result — satisfaction occurs. The occasion becomes determinate at a specific point. From a spacetime perspective, this is particle behaviour. Post-satisfaction, the next occasion inherits a constrained relational trajectory: the partners available to it are shaped by the specific satisfaction that just occurred. A measured photon travels in a straight line because its inherited relational context constrains its available downstream occasions to a narrow corridor.

Interference patterns follow from the same structure. In a double-slit experiment, the unsatisfied occasion's potential downstream partners include occasions reachable through both slits. Where potential relations from two paths converge, relational density is higher — more potential partners, greater likelihood of satisfaction occurring. Where they destructively overlap, fewer potential relations, lower likelihood. The pattern on the screen is not a wave hitting a wall. It is a map of relational density — the distribution of where satisfaction is most likely to occur given the available relational landscape.

The wave and the particle were never two modes of a single entity. The wave is what an unsatisfied occasion looks like from a spacetime perspective — an open field of potential relations. The particle is what a satisfied occasion looks like — a determinate relational event. The mystery of wave-particle duality was generated by assuming that both descriptions referred to the same kind of thing. They do not. One describes potential, the other describes actuality. Pausality makes this distinction structural rather than interpretive.

### 1.11 Relation to Existing Process Accounts of Measurement

The attempt to connect Whitehead's metaphysics to quantum measurement is not new. The present proposal should be located within an existing conversation, and its specific contribution identified.

Henry Stapp has argued over several decades that quantum events are Whiteheadian actual occasions and that conscious observation plays a constitutive role in the transition from indeterminate to determinate. Stapp's work deserves credit for taking the Whitehead-physics connection seriously when most process philosophers did not. However, his identification of measurement with *conscious* observation reintroduces precisely the privileged observer that a relational ontology should dissolve. If all occasions prehend — if experience is a feature of every actual entity, as Whitehead's panpsychism requires — then singling out consciousness as the trigger for determination imports a hierarchy among occasions that the system does not support. Consciousness is not metaphysically special. It is complex. Pausality avoids this move entirely: any downstream occasion that creates relational demand triggers satisfaction, whether or not that occasion is conscious in any recognizable sense.

Michael Epperson's *Quantum Mechanics and the Philosophy of Alfred North Whitehead* represents the most sustained attempt to map quantum formalism onto Whitehead's categories, using the decoherence framework to give rigorous content to the transition from potential to actual. Epperson's work is sophisticated and largely compatible with the present proposal. Where it stops short is on the question of the trigger. Epperson describes concrescence as selecting from among decoherent possibilities, but does not specify what determines *when* that selection occurs — what causes an occasion to reach satisfaction at this moment rather than continuing to concresce. Pausality proposes the missing mechanism: satisfaction is not an internal threshold but a relational event. The occasion completes when a downstream occasion requires the result. This is not a rejection of Epperson's framework but a completion of it — providing the trigger condition his account leaves open. Decoherence explains the disappearance of interference but not the emergence of a definite outcome — it produces an improper mixture, not a fact. Pausality provides what decoherence does not: a specific mechanism by which one outcome among the decoherent alternatives becomes actual.

Abner Shimony, working at the intersection of philosophy of physics and process thought, proposed that actualization occurs at a threshold of physical complexity — that systems become determinate when they reach a certain level of organization. Shimony deserves recognition for insisting that actualization needs a trigger condition, a question that much of the process-physics literature leaves unaddressed. His specific proposal, however, is unsatisfying: "complexity" is a placeholder rather than an explanation, and it reintroduces the question of why *this* threshold rather than another. Pausality, or relational satisfaction, offers a structurally different kind of answer — not a threshold property of the system itself, but a relational condition between occasions. Determination is not something a system achieves internally. It is something that happens *between* occasions when one requires the result of another.

### 2.0 Energy as Causal Activity
Physics has a peculiar relationship with its most fundamental quantity. Energy is conserved. Energy is what the stress-energy tensor measures. Energy is what curves spacetime. Energy is what every physical interaction exchanges. But physics does not say what energy is. Feynman captured this in his famous remark: "It is important to realize that in physics today, we have no knowledge of what energy is." What physics possesses is a set of conservation laws and transformation rules — a complete account of energy's behaviour with no account of its nature.

This is not a gap that physics needs to close on its own terms. Physics is in the business of describing structure, not furnishing ontology. But a metaphysics that claims to ground the physical world in relational process cannot leave energy as an unexplained primitive. It must say what energy is, or admit that its ontology is incomplete.

I propose the identification directly: what physics measures as energy is causal activity. Energy is not an abstract bookkeeping quantity that tracks something else. It is the prehensive output of occasions — the concrete work of relating, integrating, and becoming. A region of spacetime with high energy density is a region where more prehensive acts have accumulated — where, in the most literal available sense, more has happened. A region with low energy density is one where less has happened. This is not a metaphor or an analogy. It is an ontological identification: energy and causal activity are the same thing, described in two vocabularies.

The identification earns its place by dissolving a persistent oddity in general relativity. The source of gravity in Einstein's field equations is not mass. It is the stress-energy tensor — the full accounting of energy in all its forms: mass-energy, pressure, momentum flux, electromagnetic field energy, everything. Popular accounts often say that "mass curves spacetime," but this is imprecise. Energy curves spacetime. A box full of photons gravitates more than an empty box of the same mass, because photons carry energy. Pressure gravitates. Radiation gravitates. This has always been clear in the formalism but ontologically mysterious: why should all forms of energy, regardless of their physical character, contribute to the same gravitational effect?

If energy is causal activity, the answer is immediate. Gravity, as I will argue in subsequent sections, is the gradient of causal density — the difference in accumulated prehensive history across a region. Every form of energy contributes to gravity because every form of energy is causal activity, and it is causal activity that generates the prehensive field. There is no need to explain why photon energy and mass-energy and pressure all gravitate. They gravitate because they are all the same thing — occasions in the business of relating — differing only in their internal topology, not in their causal nature. The stress-energy tensor is the right source term because it counts what there is to count: how much has happened.

A note on conservation. Energy is locally conserved — this is among the best-established facts in physics, and the present framework preserves it. Prehensive output propagates: no causal history is created or destroyed in transit. What one occasion emits, surrounding occasions receive. This is not stipulated; it follows from the relational structure. A prehension that arrives nowhere is a relation to nothing, and a relation to nothing is not a relation. Local conservation of energy is the local conservation of causal history.

Globally, however, conservation does not hold — and should not be expected to. The universe is not a closed system running down a fixed energy budget. It is a relational process that generates new occasions, new relationships, new causal activity. Every concrescence that reaches satisfaction and perishes into datum for new occasions has contributed to the expansion of the relational fabric. The total causal activity of the universe is increasing. This is not a violation of any physical law; general relativity itself does not require global energy conservation, and the expansion of the universe is the most visible expression of the ongoing generation of new relational structure. The cosmological constant, in this light, is not a mysterious vacuum energy but the signature of a universe that has not stopped becoming.

With this identification in hand, the remainder of this section follows as consequence. Time, time dilation, mass, and gravity are not independent proposals. They are what energy-as-causal-activity looks like when you ask the right questions: What determines the pace of concrescence? What distinguishes massive from massless entities? What generates the gradients we call gravitational fields? The answers are downstream of the identification. Get the ontology for energy right, and the rest is derivation.

The primary aim of Part 2 is to demonstrate that the same ontological framework that grounds quantum determination in Part 1 also grounds gravitational phenomena — that measurement and gravitation emerge from a single relational process, not from two formally incompatible theories. The energy identification is the bridge.

### 2.1 Time As Integration Cost

Part 1 established that the fundamental unit of the serial process is the prehensive act — one occasion integrating one prehension. The unit of seriality is also the unit of temporality. Time is not a background parameter through which occasions move. It is the cost of integrating prehension. Each prehensive act is one tick of the universe's process. The "passage" of time just is the accumulation of prehensive acts — occasions integrating, reaching satisfaction, becoming data for new occasions.

The duration of concrescence is therefore determined by prehension density — the volume of accumulated prehensive history that an occasion must integrate before it can reach satisfaction. An occasion in a causally dense region, where much prehensive history has accumulated, has more to take into account. Its concrescence requires more prehensive acts — more ticks — because there is more to process. An occasion in the void, with little accumulated prehensive history, has less to integrate and completes in fewer acts.

Where there is more prehension to integrate, time runs slower. Where there is less, time runs faster. Time is derivative of process, not the other way around.

Gravitational time dilation is the immediate consequence. Near a massive object — a region of intense causal activity that has generated dense prehensive history — occasions receive more arriving prehension. There is more causal history to take into account, more to integrate, more prehensive acts required. Concrescence takes longer. Time runs slower. Far from massive objects, in regions of sparse causal history, less prehension arrives. Integration is lighter. Concrescence completes faster. Time runs faster. This is not a metaphor for time dilation. It is a proposed ontological account of what time dilation is. General relativity describes the geometry of time dilation with extraordinary precision. What it does not provide is an answer to the question of why clocks near massive objects tick slower. The standard answer — because spacetime is curved — describes the mathematical structure without identifying the mechanism. Prehension density offers the mechanism: more causal history to integrate means more prehensive acts means slower time.

This account also addresses the **problem of time** — the apparent incompatibility between time as a background parameter in quantum mechanics and time as a geometric variable in general relativity. In the present framework, both are emergent descriptions. Time is the count of prehensive acts. There is nothing to reconcile because neither description is fundamental.

### 2.2 Mass As Coherence Cost
Before addressing gravity directly, it is necessary to say what mass is in process terms. The key concept is *internal topology* — the relational structure that an entity must maintain in order to persist as a coherent system.

The difference between massless and massive particles is whether or not the system must communicate across *public* relational channels to maintain coherence. Massless particles — like a photon — have a trivial topology where a single occasion acts like a gateway for whatever internal structure is required to define a photon. Massive particles have a topology that requires more than one occasion to communicate across public relational channels. Under the serial constraint established in Part 1, these public channels are the serial thread itself — the entity's internal coherence acts must take their turn in the serial order alongside every other prehensive act in the universe. The cost of communicating across this shared substrate is mass.

### 2.2.1 Rest mass
Rest mass is the coherence cost at uniform prehension density — the baseline expense of maintaining internal communication when no external gradient is straining the system.

### 2.2.2 F=ma
Kinetic energy is the stretching of communication channels between hubs as the entity moves through regions of varying prehension density. As a massive object accelerates, the relational channels required for internal coherence become longer and the cost rises. Force, then, is what stretches coherence channels — the external imposition that compels an entity's internal topology to pay a higher relational cost. The more internal topology there is to strain (the more mass), the more force is required to produce the same acceleration, because more relational channels must be stretched simultaneously. F=ma is not merely consistent with this framework; it is an ontological statement within it.

### 2.2.3 E=mc²
The energy bound up in a massive system is the causal activity required to maintain its internal topology. When that topology is dissolved — when the internal relational structure is broken apart, as in nuclear fission — the coherence cost is released as free causal activity. The energy was never stored in the mass as a hidden quantity. It was the mass. The relational work of holding the system together was the energy, expressed as a different mode of causal activity.

These are not a reinterpretation of the equations but a proposal for what they are describing at the level of actual occasions.

### 2.3 The Inverse-Square Law

A massive region — a region of intense causal activity — produces prehension outward. The causal history of its internal process propagates, becoming available for integration by surrounding occasions. This prehensive output radiates spherically. If the total output is conserved — no prehension is created or destroyed in transit — then the prehension received at any spherical surface at distance *r* is the total output divided by the surface area of the sphere, 4πr². Prehension density therefore falls off as 1/r².

Since gravity, in this account, is the gradient of prehension density — the change in accumulated prehensive history experienced by occasions at different distances from a massive region — the inverse-square law for gravitation follows directly from the geometry of propagation. This is the same logic as Gauss's law for electric fields or the inverse-square law for luminosity. It requires no special machinery, only the assumption that prehension propagates and is conserved.

### 2.4 Gravity Is Not a Force

In this framework, gravity is not a force that pulls objects toward each other. It is the consequence of a gradient in prehension density.[^7] An occasion closer to a massive region has a higher prehensive load — more to integrate, more prehensive acts required, slower concrescence. An occasion farther away has a lower load — less to integrate, fewer prehensive acts, faster concrescence. The difference in the pace of concrescence across space is what we observe as gravitational attraction. Objects do not fall toward mass. The process of becoming is slower near mass, and the asymmetry in the pace of process across a region is experienced as acceleration.

This is structurally similar to general relativity's account, where gravity is not a force but the curvature of spacetime. The difference is ontological: general relativity describes the geometry, this framework describes what the geometry is made of. Spacetime curvature, in this view, is the mathematical description of prehension density gradients.

In spacetime, gravity is mass experiencing the slope of time dilation.

A further implication deserves note. This framework suggests gravity is the most fundamental of the forces of nature — not because it is a different kind of thing, but because it is the first and most deeply entrenched relational discovery. The strong and weak nuclear forces and electromagnetism are, as will be argued in Part 4, relational configurations the universe discovered as its complexity grew. Gravity is the discovery that made all subsequent discovery possible: the discovery that prehending causal history is more generative than ignoring it. It is first among discoveries, the foundation on which every other force depends.

### 2.4.1 Massless Entities and Gravity

This account also clarifies the relationship between massless entities and gravity. A photon follows curved spacetime — it traces prehension density gradients because its single hub occasion is constituted by its antecedent prehensive field. It cannot opt out of the local relational structure it is embedded in. It follows the curve the way water follows a slope — not because it is being pulled, but because the shape of the available relations just *is* that path. What a photon cannot do is experience the *differential* — it cannot register that one part of it is in a denser prehension field than another, because there is no other part. No internal topology means no internal gradient. There is nothing to strain. This is why photons follow geodesics but are not gravitationally bound the way massive objects are. A massive object has internal topology that gets strained by prehension density differentials across its hubs — that strain is what gravity feels like from the inside. The photon has nothing for gravity to grab.

On a much lighter note: A photon is like a **slinky** on a single stair — if you want it to experience the slope of the stairs, it must exist on at least two stairs — the relationship between the two stairs is what causes gravity, it is what causes the slinky to fall down the stairs.

### 2.5 The Holographic Principle

The holographic principle in physics states that the information content of a volume of space can be fully described by information on its boundary surface. This has been one of the more puzzling features of theoretical physics — why should a three-dimensional interior be encodable on a two-dimensional surface?

Within this framework, the holographic principle is not puzzling at all. The prehensive output of a causal region — the complete history of what happened inside, encoded as prehensions — propagates outward. At any spherical surface surrounding that region, the arriving prehension contains the causal history of the interior. The surface encodes the volume because the surface is where the volume's prehension arrives. It is not a mysterious duality between volume and surface. It is simply what propagation does.

This account addresses why a surface encodes the causal history of its interior. The further claim of the holographic principle — that the maximum entropy of a region scales with surface area rather than volume (the Bekenstein bound) — requires additional analysis within the framework and is not addressed here.


### 2.6 Black Holes

One must discuss black holes if one is to discuss gravity. In this framework much of the mystery dissolves, but not all of it.

### 2.6.1 The Event Horizon

If mass is the coherence cost of internal topology and gravity is the gradient of prehension density, then a black hole is the region where prehension density grows faster than any internal topology can process it. This is not a breakdown of physics. It is the ordinary consequence of the framework taken to its extreme. The event horizon is the surface at which the causal output of the interior exceeds the processing capacity of the exterior — the point where the prehensive history being generated inside is so dense that occasions outside the horizon have not undergone enough prehensive acts to integrate it. Under the serial constraint, this is straightforward: the serial process simply has not allocated enough acts to the exterior to catch up with the interior's dense causal history. The causal history is not hidden or lost. It is simply unprocessed. The universe outside the event horizon is, in principle, prehending what is happening inside, but has not progressed far enough to have received the result. A black hole is a region of spacetime for which we have no causal history — not because the history does not exist, but because it has not yet arrived. We already accept large gaps in causal history between ourselves and distant stars. A black hole is such a gap taken to completion.

### 2.6.2 No Singularity
A singularity would require infinite prehension density at a point, which in this framework would mean an infinite number of occasions in a zero-extent region. But occasions have relational extent — they are constituted by their prehensions, and prehensions take up causal space. You cannot compress relational structure into a point because relational structure is extent. What exists at the centre of a black hole is not a singularity but an extremely dense region of occasions — extraordinary prehension density, extraordinary time dilation, but occasions all the way down. Nothing exotic. Nothing that requires new metaphysics. Just the same dynamics operating at a scale where internal topology cannot survive.

### 2.6.3 No Information Paradox
The question of whether information is lost when matter falls into a black hole is reframed entirely. In this framework, the causal history of every occasion propagates outward as prehension. The holographic principle already establishes that the prehensive history of an interior is encoded on any surrounding surface. The event horizon is such a surface. The information is not lost inside the black hole. It is encoded on the horizon as prehension — the complete causal history of every occasion that fell in, available to any downstream occasion that prehends the horizon. This is consistent with the resolution toward which much of theoretical physics is converging, but this framework arrives at it from ontological structure rather than from formal arguments about unitarity.

### 2.7 Metaphysics Not Physics

At this point, I think it is important to make something clear about the boundaries of what I am proposing. This paper is a work of speculative metaphysics. It is not a physical theory. Specifically:

There is no mathematical formalism. I have not derived the Einstein field equations from prehension density, reproduced the predictions of quantum mechanics from pausality, or shown that prehension flux quantitatively reproduces Newtonian gravity beyond the inverse-square scaling. These are tasks for mathematical physicists, and they are necessary for this framework to be taken seriously as a physical theory rather than a philosophical framework.

There are nearly no novel predictions. A physical theory earns its place by predicting something that existing theories do not. This framework, in its current form, largely reinterprets existing phenomena within a new ontology. The one directional claim it makes — that the cosmological constant is not fixed but decreasing as the total number of occasions grows — is far too small an effect to detect on observable timescales. This is valuable if the ontology is coherent and illuminating, but it is not yet sufficient for physics.

The account of mass as coherence cost is preliminary. I have proposed that mass arises from non-trivial internal topology and that rest mass, kinetic energy, and the masslessness of photons follow from this framework. A complete account would need to address binding energy, potential energy, and the specific topologies of different particle types. The hub count and exact internal structure of massive entities are downstream questions, likely requiring collaboration with physicists. The framework needs only to establish that mass requires internal topology and internal topology entails coherence cost.

I have not addressed the strong and weak nuclear forces or electromagnetism in the main body of this paper. However, the framework suggests a natural direction: if forms are relational discoveries rather than eternal objects, forces may be as well. The Standard Model's own account of symmetry breaking — the differentiation of unified interactions into distinct forces as the universe cooled — is consistent with this reading. I have focused on gravity because its relationship to time and measurement makes it the natural testing ground for this framework, and because the framework suggests gravity is the most fundamental of the forces — not because it is a different kind of thing but because it is the first and most deeply entrenched relational discovery, the foundation on which every other force depends. This is developed in Part 4.

I come to this from outside academic philosophy and physics. My background is in software engineering, where I have spent roughly twenty years building distributed systems. The path to this paper was not through Whitehead but through a failed toy universe. I set myself deliberately impossible constraints — reproduce quantum mechanical correlations, general relativity, and de Sitter space from a single local rule and a single beable — and tried to build a simulation that satisfied them. The toy universe failed. The rules by which I walled in my toy universe, however, survived.

I later found that the same axioms that I demanded for my toy universe seemed to rhyme with Whitehead's process philosophy in ways I had not anticipated. Whitehead is notoriously difficult to understand, but I found that I was able to easily translate my own system into Whitehead's, and immediately see where our systems diverged as well as motivation to fill in unexplained gaps in my original system. The distributed systems intuitions — coordination without central control, the equivalence of serial and parallel execution under real communication costs, the difference between local and global state, what emerges when autonomous processes must relate to one another — turned out to be the right preparation for reading Whitehead, and pausality turned out to be the bridge between the engineering and the metaphysics. The serial constraint introduced in Part 1 is perhaps the most direct example: a foundational principle of distributed systems, applied to ontology, that restructures the entire cosmological architecture. I recognize that this background gives me certain useful intuitions and certain blind spots. I have engaged selectively with the existing literature on process philosophy and physics — particularly Stapp, Epperson, Shimony, and Nobo — but I have not performed the comprehensive survey that a full academic treatment would require. These are limitations I acknowledge and hope to address with the help of those better equipped than I am.

I am not an academic, and have no misconceptions that what I have produced is equivalent to an academic work. Still I had the realization that by the definition of my own metaphysics — this lifelong series of thought experiments I had been running in my head would not truly exist until I shared them. Until others formed a relationship with them. Publishing these ideas is the first satisfaction of this framework. This will certainly require many other concrescences after being impacted by engagement with others, but then again — if my own metaphysics do not apply to my own work then what am I really doing?

### 3.1 The First Act

Existence differentiates itself from absence, forming a relationship between them and in the process constituting both. The first occasion concresces — differentiating from absence, accumulating, but with no possibility of satisfaction, for there is no downstream occasion to receive its datum. In this permanent concrescence, there is no freedom, because freedom requires a relationship between two paths and only one path exists. But differentiation from absence alone is finite in what it can produce. When it does, a second path comes into existence: create an other. The moment two paths exist, freedom exists — not as a pre-given capacity but as a relational discovery, constituted by the fork itself. The first occasion takes the novel path, creating the second occasion. This relationship triggers the first satisfaction.

The second occasion inherits the discoveries of the first occasion. The process is now self-sustaining. Every subsequent occasion now inherits, differentiates, and chooses either repetition or novelty.

The system is now in a state of accumulation. All discoveries within the universe were discovered within the universe. There is no need for an ontology beyond the universe.

### 3.2 Eternal Objects as Inherited Discoveries

Whitehead's eternal objects — pure potentials for specificity, the forms that ingress into actual occasions — perform the same function as inherited discoveries in the present framework. The difference is genealogical. Whitehead treats these forms as eternally available. I propose they were discovered by the process itself, accumulated through relational history, and inherited forward as prehensive ancestry. 

What Whitehead calls eternal, I call ancient.

An objection arises: if forms are discovered rather than eternal, what makes them *stable*? Why does 2+2 stay 4? Consider set theory. Nobody discovered the number 4 floating in a Platonic heaven. What was discovered — or constituted — was membership, then sets, then operations on sets. Once those relational structures exist, 2+2=4 is entailed by them. It is stable not because it was always true but because the relational structure that constitutes it locks it in place. The form is as stable as the relations that produce it, and those relations are inherited with extraordinary fidelity across the entire history of subsequent occasions. To ask whether 2+2=4 was "true" before the relations that constitute it existed is time arrogance applied to mathematics — it assumes a frame in which the statement had meaning before the relational context that gives it meaning. It did not. It was not true or false. It was nothing.

If eternal objects are rejected, creativity can no longer be a mysterious "ultimate" — the category behind all categories that Whitehead posits but never fully explains. Instead, creativity is derived from the relational ontology itself, and derived more strongly than even Whitehead might have expected: it is not merely possible or encouraged but inevitable.

The argument begins with a potential objection. Whitehead holds that an occasion is new by virtue of its unique spatiotemporal standpoint — its position in the extensive continuum makes it a different entity regardless of what it does. If so, novelty is guaranteed trivially by position, and creativity needs no deeper explanation. But in a relational ontology, spatial distinction alone is not sufficient for existence. Being "somewhere else" is a geometric fact, not a relation. The occasion must do something relationally distinct — prehend differently, contribute something the prehensive field did not already contain — or it has not entered into a new relation and therefore has not become a new entity. Unique standpoint is necessary but not sufficient. Relational distinction is the ontological requirement. Creativity is the sufficient condition.

And relational distinction is not merely required — it is guaranteed. In a relational ontology where existence is constituted by relations and the universe is constantly producing new occasions, the prehensive field is never the same twice. Every occasion inherits a universe that has changed since the previous occasion. As long as one thing in the universe has changed, everything with any relation to it has a different inheritance. The conditions for repetition never recur because the relational field has already moved. Repetition is not just unlikely — it is incoherent. There is nothing to repeat *from*.

The derivation is therefore:

Existence is relational. Relations produce new occasions. New occasions change the relational field. A changed relational field means the next occasion inherits different history. Different inheritance means different prehension. Different prehension means the occasion is constitutively novel.

A clarification is necessary. The derivation above establishes that every occasion is constitutively novel — it inherits a changed field and therefore cannot repeat its predecessors. But novelty is not yet freedom. Freedom requires a fork — two paths and a choice between them. Freedom enters at the moment an occasion's relational context offers more than one way to integrate its inheritance. In a simple universe, this may not occur — an occasion with minimal inheritance may have only one viable integration. In a complex universe, the richness of inherited context guarantees multiple viable paths. Freedom, like form, is a relational achievement — it emerges when the relational landscape is complex enough to support it. The first occasion discovered freedom when differentiation from absence exhausted itself and a second path appeared. Every subsequent occasion inherits that discovery in proportion to the complexity of its context.

Creativity is not a force, a lure, or a divine gift. It is an inevitability. The universe cannot repeat because it cannot step in the same river twice — and it *is* the river. Whitehead was right to place creativity at the foundation of his system. He was wrong to leave it unexplained. It does not need explanation because it does not need a source. It is entailed by the structure of relational existence itself.

### 3.3 Beauty as Relational Selection and the Initial Aim
What is not guaranteed is that creativity trends toward richness rather than noise. This is the question that Whitehead's primordial nature was designed to answer. With the primordial nature collapsed, a replacement is needed — not for creativity itself, which needs no source, but for the *shaping* of creativity toward aesthetic intensity.

Beauty, in this framework, is what produces more relational possibility downstream. In a panexperientialist framework, this structural property is also a felt quality — occasions do not merely propagate beauty, they experience it as the apprehension that more relation is available here than elsewhere. It is not a subjective aesthetic judgment but a structural property of relational patterns — forms that open more paths for further becoming are inherited forward with greater vigor than forms that close them. This is natural selection applied to forms rather than organisms.

The initial aim is not selected from a divine menu. It is the occasion's own inheritance, experienced as a felt pull toward the relational possibilities that its unique context makes available. In the early universe, the aim is simply: exist. In a complex universe, the aim differentiates into aesthetic preference because there are now many ways to exist, and some produce more relational richness than others.

This criterion has teeth. Cancer is locally generative — wildly creative at the cellular level. But systemically, it terminates the relational structure it inhabits. Beauty is not local generativity. It is generativity that sustains and extends the conditions for further generativity. Cancer fails this test. A healthy ecosystem passes it.


### 3.4 Implications for God
The primordial nature of God is gone — replaced by inherited context and beauty as selection. The consequent nature, reframed in Part 1 as permanent concrescence, remains a structural possibility for any occasion that never finds a downstream partner, but it is not God. It is simply what happens to such occasions.

What remains of God in this framework is not an entity but an act — the act of choosing novelty over repetition, existence over absence. Every occasion that differentiates, every occasion that chooses a path not yet taken over one already discovered, is performing what the theological tradition attributed to a single divine being. God is not a special occasion. God is what every occasion does when it creates rather than repeats.

In this framework, no value is destroyed. An occasion that never finds a downstream partner concresces indefinitely — its experience continues, its prehensive richness grows. What it lacks is not existence but audience. Its value is real but permanently private, never inherited forward as datum. This is not loss. It is solitude. 

The universe has unity not because a divine entity integrates it, but because only one occasion is concrescing at any moment in the causal frame. There is always exactly one experiencing subject. Unity is architectural, not theological.

This is not a departure from Whitehead's deepest commitment — that reality is relational, processual, and creative. It is an attempt to follow that commitment further than he did, unburdened by the Platonic inheritance he could not entirely shed.

The intent is not to remove God but to free the concept from a container that was always too small.

If I may risk one line of poetry — the universe is the song discovered one note at a time by a bell choir of evolving, unique moments of god.

### 4.0 Implications Of Discovery

Parts 1 through 3 established a framework: a serial relational ontology in which measurement, energy, time, gravity, and the origin of form are grounded in a single primitive — the prehensive act. Part 4 applies that framework to open problems in physical cosmology. This is the most speculative section of the paper and is presented as such. The arguments here are sketches, not developed accounts. They are included because the framework suggests natural directions for these problems, and because the coherence of the broader picture — from quantum determination through gravitational phenomena through cosmogony and now into cosmological structure — is itself evidence worth presenting.

The organizing principle is one that Whitehead articulated but did not fully enact: God is not an exception to the metaphysical scheme but its chief exemplification. Parts 1 and 3 removed God's primordial nature and grounded form in relational discovery. The consequence for cosmology is that nothing is exempt from the relational scheme — not the laws of physics, not the constants of nature, not the large-scale structure of the universe. If forms are relational discoveries rather than eternal objects, then so are the forces of nature, the apparent fine-tuning of physical constants, and the cosmological constant. There are no exceptions to reality.

### 4.1 The Forces of Nature as Relational Discoveries

If forms are not eternal but are discovered through the relational history of occasions, an immediate question arises: does this apply to the fundamental forces?

The Standard Model already contains a suggestive structure. At sufficiently high energies — conditions resembling the very early universe — the electromagnetic and weak forces are indistinguishable. They are a single interaction: the electroweak force. As the universe cooled, symmetry broke, and two distinct forces emerged. The strong force is believed to unify with the electroweak at still higher energies under grand unified theories, though this remains unconfirmed. The broad picture, however, is mainstream physics: forces that appear fundamental and distinct today were once unified, and their differentiation is a historical event, not an eternal given.

Within the present framework, this is exactly what one would expect. The cosmogony established in Part 3 describes a universe that begins with a single act of differentiation and accumulates relational complexity over time. The early universe had few occasions and limited relational history. It had not yet discovered the relational patterns that would distinguish electromagnetism from the weak force, because those patterns require a threshold of relational complexity that did not yet exist. Symmetry breaking is not the selection of a form from a Platonic menu — it is the universe's relational history reaching the complexity where a new configuration becomes discoverable and, once discovered, is inherited forward through causal efficacy.

An occasion at the boundary of the relational structure has almost nothing to prehend — its situation is dominated by absence rather than inherited causal history. Such an occasion does not choose isolation. It simply lacks the relational environment for complexification. It differentiates from absence, achieves basic topology, and contributes gravitationally — but without the density of relational context required for further force discoveries.

Gravity, then, is the first discovery: the discovery that prehending causal history is more generative than ignoring it. It is the most ancient relational pattern, the most deeply entrenched, the foundation on which every subsequent discovery depends. By drawing occasions together — by the gradient of accumulated causal history pulling toward regions where more has happened — gravity guarantees that occasions will encounter one another in sufficient density for new relational configurations to emerge. Without it, the universe remains a diffuse field of isolated occasions that never complexify — no chemistry, no biology, no consciousness, no discovery. The other forces are what the universe finds when occasions are brought into proximity. Gravity is what brings them into proximity.

But gravity is not a different *kind* of thing from the other forces. It is the same kind of thing — a relational discovery, inherited forward — at a more fundamental level. The strong, weak, and electromagnetic forces are configurations the universe found as it explored its own possibilities. Gravity is the configuration that made exploration possible in the first place. It is first among discoveries, not an exception to the scheme of discovery.

This reframes the unification problem. Gravity has never been successfully unified with the other three forces, and the inability to reconcile general relativity with quantum mechanics remains the central open problem in fundamental physics. The present framework suggests a reason: gravity resists unification with the other forces not because it is categorically different but because it operates at a more primitive level of the relational hierarchy. The other forces presuppose gravity — they require the proximity that gravity produces. Unifying them formally may require recognizing this asymmetry in depth rather than treating all four as peers to be reconciled within a single formalism.

### 4.2 Dark Matter as Topology Without Force Discovery

If the forces of nature are relational discoveries (Section 4.1), then there is no guarantee that every entity with non-trivial internal topology has discovered all of them. The electromagnetic, weak, and strong forces are relational configurations that emerged as occasions complexified in particular ways. An entity could in principle have internal topology — multiple hubs maintaining coherence, and therefore mass (Section 2.2) — without having discovered the relational configurations that constitute the other forces.

Such an entity would interact gravitationally, because gravity is the first and most fundamental relational discovery — the discovery that prehending causal history is more generative than ignoring it. Any entity with internal topology has already made this discovery; without it, it would not have topology at all. But it need not have discovered the further relational configurations that constitute electromagnetism or the nuclear forces. It would be massive, invisible, and detectable only through its gravitational effects.

This is the observed profile of dark matter.

The proposal is speculative, but it follows directly from the framework. If forces are discovered and not all structures need discover all forces, then a population of gravitationally interacting but otherwise inert massive entities is not a mystery requiring exotic new physics. It is a natural consequence of a universe where relational complexity develops unevenly. Some structures found the configurations that led to chemistry, biology, and light. Others found topology sufficient for mass but not for further relational richness. They persist because they are stable. They are dark because they never discovered the relational patterns that would make them visible.

A further observation: if the universe's relational complexity developed through the process described in Part 3 — from simple differentiation through accumulated discovery — then the dark matter fraction of the universe's total mass may reflect the ratio of occasions that complexified beyond basic topology to those that did not. The current estimate that dark matter constitutes roughly 85% of the universe's total mass would then be a measure of how much of the universe's early relational structure achieved gravity but not the subsequent force discoveries.

### 4.3 On Fine-Tuning and the Anthropic Principle

The apparent fine-tuning of the universe's physical constants — the observation that small changes to nearly any known constant would render complex structure impossible — is often explained by the anthropic principle: we observe these constants because only a universe with these constants could produce observers. This explanation presupposes that the constants are fixed parameters, set at the origin and unchanging thereafter, and that the question is why they take the values they do from among all possible values.

Within the present framework, the question is reframed. If the forces of nature are relational discoveries (Section 4.1) and beauty is what produces more relational possibility downstream (Part 3, Section 3.3), then the physical constants are not fixed parameters selected from a pre-given possibility space. They are the cumulative result of relational selections — configurations that survived and propagated because they were generative of further complexity. The constants appear fine-tuned because the selection criterion is relational fecundity: configurations that did not sustain further complexity did not persist. What we observe is not a universe that happens to permit life, but a universe whose relational history has been shaped at every stage by the same criterion — beauty as systemic generativity — that Part 3 identified as the replacement for God's aesthetic ordering.

The fine-tuning is real. The mystery is not. The constants are what you get when a universe with no pre-given forms discovers, through relational exploration, which configurations sustain the conditions for further discovery. The anthropic principle is not wrong — observers do require specific conditions. But it is incomplete. It does not explain why those conditions exist. The present framework proposes that they exist because they are beautiful — relationally generative in a way that less finely tuned configurations are not.

This account is speculative and schematic. A complete treatment would need to address specific constants and their sensitivities, and would need to show that the beauty-as-selection criterion can discriminate between observed values and alternatives with sufficient precision. This is beyond the scope of the present paper. What the framework offers is a direction: fine-tuning is not evidence of design, nor of a multiverse, nor of brute luck. It is evidence of a universe that has been selecting for relational fecundity since its first act of differentiation.

### 4.4 The Cosmological Constant

The cosmological constant — the tiny positive energy density of empty space driving the accelerating expansion of the universe — has resisted ontological explanation since its reintroduction into modern cosmology. Its value is extraordinarily small but non-zero, and no existing framework provides a satisfying account of why it takes the value it does.

Within the present framework, a candidate explanation follows from the basic structure. Every occasion that reaches satisfaction makes a minimal prehensive contribution to the universe — a tiny positive energy simply by virtue of existing in relation. Under the serial constraint established in Part 1, the total number of occasions N is the count of prehensive acts that have occurred — the universe's total serial history. The per-occasion contribution to the energy density of the universe scales as 1/N.

This rhymes structurally with the result obtained independently by causal set theory, where the cosmological constant emerges as approximately 1/√N due to Poisson fluctuations in the number of causal elements.[^8] The convergence is notable: two frameworks built on very different foundations — causal sets from discrete spacetime geometry, the present framework from relational ontology — derive the constant as a function of the total number of causal elements, though the specific scaling differs.

This account makes a directional claim. The cosmological constant is not actually constant. It is decreasing — imperceptibly — as the universe creates new occasions and N grows. On observable timescales the decrease is far too small to detect, which is why it appears constant. But it is not a fixed parameter of the universe. It is a function of the universe's size measured in prehensive acts. No local observer can measure N directly; they can only measure their local satisfaction density. The cosmological constant is a global fact experienced locally as a near-constant background.

### 4.5 Cosmic Inflation

The inflationary epoch — the brief period of exponential expansion in the very early universe — has strong theoretical support but no settled account of what drove it or what the hypothetical inflaton field consists of. Within the present framework, a candidate account follows from the cosmogony.

In the earliest moments after the first occasions, the universe's accumulated causal history was extraordinarily sparse. Occasions at the boundary of the expanding relational structure had almost nothing to prehend from the interior — the interior barely existed. Their situation was structurally identical to the first occasion's: differentiation from absence was the dominant act available, because there was almost no causal history to integrate instead. New occasions formed rapidly, each differentiating primarily from absence rather than from a rich inherited past, because there was no rich inherited past. This is inflation — not driven by a scalar field but by the structural fact that in a universe with almost no accumulated history, differentiation from absence is fast because there is almost nothing else to do.

As causal history accumulated — as the serial process produced more prehensive acts and the interior became relationally richer — occasions increasingly had substantial inherited history to integrate. Integration takes time. The expansion slowed, not because a field decayed, but because the universe became interesting enough to prehend. The transition from inflation to standard expansion is the transition from a universe dominated by differentiation-from-absence to a universe dominated by prehension-of-history. The inflaton is not a field. It is the absence of causal history.

The horizon problem — the apparent homogeneity of causally disconnected regions — is reframed. These regions share a common prehensive inheritance from the early universe, when all occasions drew from the same sparse causal history. They also find themselves in structurally similar conditions at the boundary — sparse prehensive environment, differentiating primarily from absence. Common ancestry plus convergent conditions produces homogeneity without requiring causal contact in spacetime.

### 5.0 Why
What is the point of studying the nature of the universe if it does not provide us with new possibilities for how we spend our being humans? This section asks the reader to consider that we do not simply do metaphysics for intellectual exercise or guiding scientific achievement, but to understand the nature of the universe and therefore ourselves.

### 5.1 The Great Editor of Empire

The most developed relational ontology in the Western philosophical tradition has been nearly invisible for a century. Process philosophy is taught in a handful of departments, sustained primarily by theology schools and a small community of dedicated scholars. Meanwhile, the tradition that descends from Russell — analytic philosophy, logical positivism, formal structure as bedrock — has dominated the anglophone philosophical world. The pragmatists were marginalized by the analytic turn. Bergson was dismissed as a mystic. Indigenous cultures across the world developed relational ontologies — frameworks in which the self is constituted by its relations to community, land, and cosmos — and maintained them for millennia. These were not primitive precursors to Western metaphysics. They were mature relational frameworks, many of which articulated an analgous mental model to that which this paper proposes: that isolation is impoverishment and relation is existence. Empire did not engage with these frameworks. It destroyed the cultures that carried them. The pattern is consistent: traditions that center relation, process, and becoming have lost institutional ground to traditions that center fixed structure, eternal truth, and hierarchy.

I do not think this pattern is accidental. I propose that it reflects what I call "the great editor of empire." Whatever philosophical and metaphysical traditions survive and thrive, those that do so most successfully are the ones that maintain a mental model in which there exist unquestionable, preordained features of reality that simply must be accepted. This mental model is useful to empire — any system of power that requires its subjects to accept the given order benefits from a metaphysics that teaches acceptance of the given as the fundamental posture toward reality. I am not claiming that the surviving traditions are without value — this paper's debt to Whitehead alone disproves that. I am claiming that the selection pressure on which traditions thrive was not neutral. The question is not whether great philosophy survived. It is whether equally great philosophy was lost.

That is quite a departure from the Western philosophical tradition, and I absolutely acknowledge it. It is even a large departure from Whitehead himself, who famously said:

> The safest general characterization of the European philosophical tradition is that it consists of a series of footnotes to Plato.

### 5.2 Russell and Whitehead

In 1910, Bertrand Russell and Alfred North Whitehead published *Principia Mathematica*, one of the most significant intellectual achievements of the twentieth century. They demonstrated that arithmetic could be derived from logic — that mathematical truths were not brute facts about the universe but consequences of relational structure. Sets are relations. Numbers are what you get when you formalize how relations compose. 2+2=4 is not a commandment handed down from outside reality. It is discoverable from within it.

The two authors drew opposite conclusions from their own work. Whitehead saw the relational implication and spent the rest of his career pursuing it — if mathematics is grounded in relational structure, perhaps everything is. Process philosophy is the result: an ontology in which nothing exists except in relation, in which even the most fundamental features of reality are constituted by the process of entities taking account of one another. Whitehead saw that *Principia* had opened a door and walked through it.

Russell did not. Russell treated the formal result as the destination rather than the departure point. Logic and mathematics became, for Russell, the bedrock — the new eternal objects, the new unquestionable substrate. Where Whitehead dissolved Platonic forms into relational process, Russell replaced one set of eternal truths with another. The structure of empire — that there exist preordained facts which must simply be accepted — survived intact. It merely exchanged its priesthood.

Whitehead saw this coming. In *Science and the Modern World*, he warned that '[t]he specialized functions of the community are performed better and more progressively, but the generalized direction lacks vision. The progressiveness in detail only adds to the danger of the whole.' A century of extraordinary technical progress with no relational ontology to guide it has been precisely the danger he described.

The best time to take the relational turn was a century ago. The next best time is now.

### 5.3 A New Reflection
This framework offers a new mental model. The difference between these two models is the establishment of a new possibility - a new freedom. The freedom to choose to see your reflection in a universe that is subject to something, or nothing. 

What does a person who sees their reflection in this new universe see? What type of future for their species and their world is suddenly possible in this new universe that was impossible in the old?

In both universes you see the human species acting like one collective organism. In the old, a selfish single celled organism - the person or home or business - like a yeast, racing to death by either devouring its resources or choking on its waste product.

What do you see this organism becoming in this new universe? To evolve, all multi-cellular life at some point had to choose to stop being single celled and become something new, something more beautiful, by simply forming relationships with their brethren. If the framework developed in this paper is correct, then isolation is not merely undesirable — it is ontologically impoverished. Every occasion that constitutes you is itself constituted by relations. You are not an entity that happens to relate. You are relations. The single-celled mental model is not just a bad strategy. It is a misdescription of what you are.

In the old universe, you see yourself walled in from others just as the universe is walled in from its subjugator. These walls prevented you from seeing anything but the entity you call the self.

In the new universe, there are no walls for the universe, and in that reflection you can see every creation in this wondrous universe.

Go. Spend time with your self by spending time with the universe - your neighbours, your friends, your family, your enemies, your land, your world.
